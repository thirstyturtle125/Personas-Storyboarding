Amazon Self-Service Lockers

  • The Amazon Self-Service Locker is an automated, secure, self-service kiosk that customers can pick up their Amazon packages from.
  • All Amazon Lockers offer accessibility features including talking lockers, larger and high contrast touchscreen text, and lower locker slot preference when needed.
  • The interface includes a touchscreen, a speaker, a barcode scanner, a keypad,1 and the lockers themselves.
  • This interface lets users pick up their packages on their schedule, not the arcane and unknowable schedule that governs front door delivery.

Key Observations

  • Security seemed to be a big reason to use an Amazon Locker.
    • Better than a front door or porch.
  • Lockers may be out of the way, or there may not be any nearby.
  • Some things do not fit in the lockers.
  • Accessibility for the disabled is an important design feature.

Questions & Responses

I ordered three things on Amazon and had them delivered to an Amazon Locker.

I asked three of my friends to pick these up and asked them these questions afterwards:

  1. What was your first impression of this interface?
      2. A: Very straightforward, although the design is not ideally aesthetic.
  2. How easy was the interface to use?
      3. A: Very easy! Takes less than a minute to use.
  3. What was the one thing you liked most about this design?
      4. A: Simplicity and accessibility were mentioned.
  4. What was the one thing you disliked most about this design?
      5. A: A majority of the lockers are very small sizes; busy areas will likely have bigger lockers in use; the whole thing is kind of an eyesore.
  5. Would you recommend the Amazon Locker to friends/family?
      6. A: Yes for convenience or if they've had problems with package security.
